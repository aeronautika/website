Discover Aeronautical Engineering & Aviation

A beginner-friendly introduction to how aircraft fly, how they are engineered, and why general aviation aircraft like Cessna and Piper are the backbone of aviation.

What is Aeronautical Engineering?

Aeronautical engineering focuses on the design, construction, testing, and operation of aircraft. It covers aerodynamics, structures, propulsion, avionics, and flight mechanics.

Aerodynamics

How air flows around wings and produces lift.

Structures

How aircraft stay strong while remaining lightweight.

Propulsion

Piston engines, turboprops, and jet engines.

General Aviation Aircraft

General aviation (GA) aircraft are commonly used for training, personal travel, and engineering fundamentals.

How to Start Learning Aviation

  • Study basic physics and mathematics
  • Learn aircraft parts and systems
  • Use flight simulators for practice
  • Read POHs (Pilot Operating Handbooks)
  • Visit local airfields and flight schools
